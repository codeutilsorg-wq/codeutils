How to Use This Tool

  1. Click "Choose PDF File" and select your PDF document

Common Use Cases

Extracting a Chapter or Section

Long reports, books, and manuals often need to be shared as smaller, focused documents. If a colleague needs only chapters 2-4 of a 200-page report, extract the relevant page range and share a compact file‚Äîwithout them having to download the entire document. Use the page range syntax to specify exactly which pages to include.

Removing Unwanted Pages Before Sharing

Documents often contain cover pages, appendices, or internal notes that should not be shared externally. Instead of editing the PDF in a complex tool, use this splitter to extract only the pages you want to share, omitting the rest. The original file is untouched‚Äîthe extracted pages go to a new file.

Creating Handouts from Presentation Slides

When a presentation PDF contains 80 slides but a handout only needs the 15 key slides, extract those specific pages by number or range. This is faster than scrolling through a presentation tool and re-exporting, and the extracted PDF retains the original slide formatting and quality.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I extract non-consecutive pages?

A: Yes. Use commas to separate individual page numbers and dashes for ranges. For example, "1, 5, 10-15, 20" extracts page 1, page 5, pages 10 through 15, and page 20‚Äîall combined into a single output PDF in that order.

Q: Does splitting affect PDF quality?

A: No. The extracted pages are taken directly from the original PDF without re-encoding or compression. Text sharpness, image quality, and formatting are fully preserved in the output file.

Q: Is my PDF safe to use with this tool?

A: Yes. All PDF processing happens entirely in your browser using JavaScript. Your file is never uploaded to any server, stored, or transmitted anywhere. This makes the tool safe for confidential legal documents, financial records, medical files, and any other sensitive content.

Q: Do I need to create an account or pay?

A: No. The tool is completely free with no registration, sign-up, or account required. There are no file size limits or page count restrictions for free users.
