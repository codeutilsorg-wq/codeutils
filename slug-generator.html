About URL Slug Generator

A URL slug is the human-readable part of a web address that identifies a specific page—the "my-blog-post-title" in https://example.com/blog/my-blog-post-title. Well-formed slugs are lowercase, hyphenated, free of special characters, and include relevant keywords for SEO. Converting a title or article name into a proper slug manually requires removing punctuation, replacing spaces with hyphens, lowercasing everything, and handling accented characters—all error-prone when done by hand. Our Slug Generator does all of this instantly, with support for international characters and multiple separator options. All processing happens in your browser.

Tips & Best Practices

  • Use hyphens, not underscores: Google officially treats hyphens as word separators in URLs, making "my-page-title" more SEO-friendly than "my_page_title". This is the most common slug convention and the tool's default
  • Keep slugs concise: Remove stop words (a, an, the, and, or, in, of) if the slug becomes too long—shorter URLs are easier to share and read. For "The Best Guide to JavaScript Frameworks", consider "best-guide-javascript-frameworks"
  • Include your primary keyword: The slug should include the main keyword of the page—this is a direct SEO ranking signal. "javascript-array-methods" is better than "post-123" or "blog-2026-02-19"
  • Avoid changing published slugs: Once a URL is indexed by Google and has backlinks, changing the slug creates a broken link and loses any accumulated SEO value—use 301 redirects if you must change a slug

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What happens to accented characters like é, ñ, or ü?

A: The tool converts accented characters to their ASCII equivalents—é becomes e, ñ becomes n, ü becomes u, and so on. This produces URL-safe slugs that work in all web contexts without percent-encoding. For example, "café résumé" becomes "cafe-resume".

Q: Should I use hyphens or underscores in URL slugs?

A: Google recommends hyphens. Their documentation explicitly states that word separators in URLs should be hyphens, not underscores, because Googlebot treats hyphens as word separators (so "my-blog-post" is read as three words) but treats underscores as word joiners (so "my_blog_post" is read as one word "myblogpost"). Always use hyphens for SEO-optimized slugs.
