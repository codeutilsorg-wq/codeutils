About Rotation Ciphers

Rotation ciphers shift characters by a fixed number of positions in the alphabet.

Cipher Types

  • ROT13: Rotates letters by 13 positions. A becomes N, B becomes O, etc. Numbers and symbols unchanged.
  • ROT5: Rotates digits by 5 positions. 0 becomes 5, 1 becomes 6, etc.
  • ROT18: Combines ROT13 (letters) and ROT5 (numbers).
  • ROT47: Rotates printable ASCII characters (33-126) by 47 positions.

Why ROT13?

ROT13 is commonly used to hide spoilers, puzzle answers, or mildly obscure text. Since 13 is half of 26 (the alphabet size), applying ROT13 twice returns the original text.
